_Decorative versions of the creaking heart and more._

## Introduction

The pale garden added some striking new blocks in the creaking heart and the eyeblossom, but they come with
gameplay baggage — creaking hearts spawn creakings, and eyeblossoms open and close (and puff pollen) on a day/night
cycle. Artificial Heart provides "tamed" decorative variants of these blocks so you can build with their look
without their behavior.

### Minecraft Versions

* 26.2: supported

### Current Features

* **Detached Creaking Heart** — a creaking heart that looks the part but is fully decorative. It does not link to a
  pale oak or spawn creakings, and its glowing "active" faces can be oriented by how it is placed.
* **Clipped Eyeblossom** and **Clipped Open Eyeblossom** — eyeblossoms frozen in the open or closed state. They no
  longer cycle between open and closed with the time of day, so the flower you place is the flower you keep.
* **Potted** variants of both clipped eyeblossoms for use in flower pots.
* Obtained by "clipping" or "detaching" the vanilla blocks with the appropriate tool (shears for eyeblossoms, an
  axe for the creaking heart), keeping the workflow intuitive and vanilla-friendly.

## Notes for Documentation

The player-facing pitch is decoration: _all the atmosphere of the pale garden, none of the spooky side effects._
When documenting in-game, the important detail is which tool converts a vanilla block into its decorative variant.
